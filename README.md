# ATL_MAPS_DRONE
An AI-powered drone system for hyperlocal air and land pollution monitoring. Using IoT sensors, computer vision, and machine learning, it identifies AQI hotspots, garbage accumulation, waste burning, and pollution sources while delivering real-time insights and alerts to citizens and municipal authorities for faster, data-driven environmental action.
## Why We Built This

This project didn't begin with a competition or an assignment—it started with a question.

One day, I noticed a group of people on a street constantly sneezing and wheezing. Out of curiosity, I checked the city's Air Quality Index, expecting it to be poor, but it was around **50**, which is generally considered "Good." It just didn't add up.

After reading more about air quality monitoring, we realised why. Most AQI systems represent the average conditions of a city or a large area. They don't tell you what's happening on the street you're standing on. A city can have a healthy average AQI while certain neighbourhoods experience much worse air due to unmanaged garbage, waste burning, heavy traffic, or other localised pollution sources.

That led us to a bigger question:

**What if we could monitor pollution at the street level instead of the city level?**

This project is our attempt to answer that question.

We developed a drone-based environmental monitoring system that combines IoT sensors, computer vision, GSM communication, and machine learning to create a hyperlocal picture of both air and land pollution. The drone measures harmful gases and particulate matter, captures images to identify waste accumulation, and uploads this information to the cloud for analysis. Machine learning models then help detect pollution patterns, estimate environmental risk, and generate useful insights.

The goal isn't just to collect data. It's to make that data useful.

Citizens can better understand the air quality around their immediate surroundings instead of relying only on city-wide averages. Municipal authorities can identify garbage hotspots, detect possible waste-burning locations, prioritize clean-up operations, and respond before problems grow larger.

We believe environmental challenges are best solved from the ground up. By giving communities a clearer picture of what's happening in their own neighbourhoods, we hope to encourage faster action, smarter decisions, and cleaner, healthier cities—one street at a time.
